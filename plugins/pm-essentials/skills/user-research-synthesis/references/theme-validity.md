# When Is a Theme Real? Synthesis Validity Rules

Synthesis fails in two directions: seeing patterns that aren't there (three loud users ≠ a theme) and flattening real signal into mush ("users want simplicity"). These are the validity rules to apply before a theme makes the report.

## The theme bar

A finding earns "theme" status only if it clears all four:

1. **Prevalence with denominators** — "6 of 14 participants" not "many users". If you can't count it, it's an anecdote (report it as one, they're allowed).
2. **Independent occurrence** — it arose unprompted in separate sessions, not after your leading question. Mark prompted findings as prompted.
3. **Behaviour over opinion** — "4 users abandoned at the export step" outranks "5 users said export matters". When words and behaviour conflict, behaviour wins and *the conflict itself is the finding*.
4. **Survives the disconfirming pass** — actively hunt the counter-evidence: who did fine at the export step? Why? A theme that ignores its exceptions becomes a bad roadmap item.

## Quote discipline

- Quotes are evidence, not decoration — each theme carries 2-3 *verbatim* quotes with participant IDs
- Never trim a quote past its qualifier ("I'd probably use it *if it synced*" — the if-clause is the data)
- One brilliant articulate participant will dominate your memory; check whose quotes you're using — if P7 appears five times, you've synthesised P7, not the study

## Segment honesty

Cut every theme by the segments that exist (role, plan, tenure). "Users hate the dashboard" that's actually "admins hate the dashboard; end-users never open it" is two different roadmaps. But: don't invent segments post-hoc to make noise look structured — segments were defined at recruitment or they're speculation (label them).

## Confidence labels for the report

- **Strong** — ≥40% of participants, behavioural, survives disconfirmation
- **Emerging** — repeated but thin (2-3), or opinion-only; worth a targeted follow-up
- **Anecdote** — once, but consequential; reported honestly as n=1
