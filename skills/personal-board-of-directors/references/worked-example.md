# Worked Example — Personal Board of Directors

## The brief

Tomás, 41, senior engineering manager at a stable fintech ($240k, low stress, mild boredom), has an offer to be VP Engineering at a 30-person Series-A healthtech ($205k + 0.6% equity, "chaotic, mission-driven"). Two kids, mortgage, partner supportive but nervous. He secretly wants to go. Decision: **take the VP role — yes or no?** Stakes: high, mostly reversible (his skills travel).

## The output

# Board Session: Take the VP Engineering offer at the Series-A — yes or no?

## Memos

**THE OPERATOR** — The first two weeks are the tell: 30 people, likely 12 engineers, no VP before you — so there's no team to inherit, there's a team to *build* while shipping. Your calendar becomes hiring (60%), incident duty, and board prep you've never done. You can execute this; you have not scheduled it. The offer letter says nothing about an EM backfill budget — get that in writing. **Vote: FOR — flips if:** they won't commit headcount for two senior hires in H1.

**THE SKEPTIC** — The kill condition isn't the company, it's the runway: Series-A healthtech, and the deck shows 19 months at current burn *before* your hires. If the next raise slips, you're doing layoffs in year two of your first VP job — that's the résumé line nobody plans for. Also: "no VP before you" means the founders have never shared control. Ask how the last senior hire left. **Vote: AGAINST — flips if:** runway ≥ 24 months post-hiring-plan, and a reference call with a departed senior hire comes back clean.

**THE CFO** — Cash comp drops $35k (−15%) against a fixed mortgage; the 0.6% at a $60M post is worth $360k *on paper*, ~$90–140k risk-adjusted over four years — roughly recovering the salary gap only in the good scenarios. This trade is negative-EV in money and positive-EV only in trajectory: VP-title compounding is real ($300k+ roles open post-success, and partially even post-failure). Numbers say: affordable, not lucrative. **Vote: ABSTAIN — flips to FOR if:** base moves to $220k or the mortgage has 6+ months' buffer saved.

**THE COACH** — You said "mild boredom" about a job you've optimized to painlessness — that's how plateaus talk. You're 41, not 51: this is likely your *best*-timed risk window, not your last one. But name the cost: two years of evenings, and your partner's "supportive but nervous" needs to become an actual conversation with numbers in it, not a mood. **Vote: FOR — flips if:** the partner conversation surfaces a hard no on the stress trade.

**THE CUSTOMER** *(the person Tomás at 50 is building for)* — He doesn't ask "did you maximize comp at 41?" He asks "did you find out whether you could build something?" A no here, with this cushion, at this age, reads to him as the decision made by the mortgage, not the man. **Vote: FOR — flips if:** the runway answer makes this a likely 18-month detour rather than a real attempt.

## Disagreement Map

| Split | Advisors | The crux | What fact resolves it |
|---|---|---|---|
| Go now vs verify first | Coach/Customer vs Skeptic | Is 19 months runway a normal Series-A number or a trap? | The post-hiring-plan runway math + next-raise timeline, from the CEO, this week |
| Money matters how much | CFO vs Customer | Whether −$35k is a cost or a tuition | The household buffer number |

## The Vote

Decision rule: **high stakes → 4-of-5 supermajority** (stated before counting). Count: **3 FOR, 1 AGAINST, 1 ABSTAIN — short of the rule.**
**Board recommendation: conditional yes — resolve the Skeptic's fact first.**
**The one question to resolve this week:** *"Walk me through runway assuming my hiring plan — and can I call the last senior person who left?"* Both answers in one call with the CEO. Good answers convert AGAINST and ABSTAIN; the rule passes 5-0 or fails honestly.

## Why it's shaped this way

- **The five voices are blind-distinguishable** — the CFO computes, the Coach quotes Tomás back to himself, the Operator schedules; forced consensus is the failure mode the skill exists against.
- **Every vote names its flip evidence** — an unflippable vote is a prejudice, per the anti-patterns.
- **The decision rule was declared before the count** and honored: 3-1-1 does NOT round up to "go."
- **The secret preference is used, not obeyed** — the board knows he wants to go; the Skeptic exists precisely because of it.
- **The one question is answerable in a single call this week** — cheap, fast, decisive; "do more research" is banned.
