# Journey Maps Built on Evidence (Not Conference-Room Fiction)

Most journey maps are what the team IMAGINES customers experience — a fiction with sticky notes. The discipline that makes a map worth the wall space: every cell traceable to evidence, and honesty about where evidence is missing.

## Evidence sources per journey element

| Element | Real source | The fiction it replaces |
|---|---|---|
| Stages & paths | Analytics flows, session recordings — where people ACTUALLY go | The funnel as designed |
| Actions | Recordings, support tickets ("I was trying to…") | "Then the user configures settings" |
| Emotions | Interview verbatims at that step, support tone, rage-clicks, NPS verbatims tagged by stage | Emoji chosen in a workshop |
| Pain points | Drop-off data + tickets + interview stories, triangulated | The team's pet grievances |
| Workarounds | Interviews + odd usage patterns (exports at step 3 = missing feature at step 3) | (usually absent entirely — and they're the gold) |

Cells without evidence stay in the map but get marked **[assumed]** — an honest map with assumption flags beats a confident fiction, and the flags become the research backlog.

## Map the divergences, not just the happy path

The single-path map hides the finding. Real journeys fork: the 60% who never reach step 3, the segment that enters sideways from an invite, the loop where people bounce between pricing and docs four times. The forks and loops ARE the insights — a map without them was drawn from the design spec, not the data.

## Emotion mapping that means something

The emotion curve earns its place when anchored: each low point carries a quote or behavioural signal ("3 of 8 interviewees described this step with anger words"; "median 4 attempts"). The curve's job is locating the moments where intervention pays — anchoring is what makes those locations trustworthy.

## From map to roadmap (or it was art)

Each major pain gets: severity (drop-off % or ticket volume) × reach (who hits this stage) × current-workaround cost. Rank. The map's output is 3-5 prioritised opportunities with evidence attached — not a poster. Schedule the map's expiry too (journeys change with every release; an 18-month-old map is a historical document).
