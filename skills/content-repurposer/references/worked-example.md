## The brief

Freightlane is a fictional B2B logistics-tech company selling quoting software to mid-size LTL (less-than-truckload) freight brokers. Episode 47 of their podcast featured their VP of Operations, Dana Okafor, on why brokers lose winnable business: across the 212 brokerages Freightlane benchmarked, the median quote response time is 3.1 hours, while the top decile answers in 22 minutes — and wins roughly twice as often on identical lanes. Marketing wants the episode atomized into a full pack. Goal: newsletter signups first, demo requests second. Voice: plainspoken operator — no growth-hack tone. Sales has asked for "book a demo" as the CTA on everything; that request conflicts with platform norms and is resolved below.

## The output

**The core idea:** Freight brokers don't lose quotes on price — they lose them on speed; the median broker answers in 3.1 hours while winners answer in 22 minutes, so the fix is an inbox workflow problem, not a pricing problem.

## Why it's shaped this way

- Everything ladders to one stated core idea (speed, not price) because the skill requires leading with "The core idea" and its quality check demands that all five pieces ladder back to it — a pack about "five things from episode 47" would be atomization without a spine.
- Each draft is genuinely rewritten, not reflowed: the thread carries one beat per tweet, LinkedIn is whitespace-heavy with a comment-bait question, the carousel keeps on-slide text under 12 words — the top anti-pattern this skill bans is "the same paragraph pasted into all five with different line breaks."
- The unverified win-rate stat in tweet 6 is flagged *(assumed — replace)* per the working-from-a-brief rule: invented specifics must be marked, never silently shipped.
- Every piece opens with a distinct earned hook ("Your cheapest quote, sent late, loses…") because the skill bans generic hooks like "Here are some thoughts on…".
- The CTA tension with sales is resolved in the open, in the cadence section, rather than sanitized away: CTAs must "match the stated goal and platform norms," and the stated primary goal was newsletter signups — so the pack overrules the blanket "book a demo" request and says why.
- LinkedIn carries exactly 4 hashtags, honouring the "no hashtag spam (3–5 max)" rule; the X thread carries none, matching platform norms.
- The cadence spreads five pieces across seven days with reasoning per slot, because the Output Format requires "which to post when, over how many days," not just a list of assets.
