The Tower of Claims

Every sentence is a block. The evidence is the material. Physics is the reviewer.

Paste a document and it becomes a tower: sentences are blocks, evidence is material — steel for data, stone for sourced claims, wood for assertions, cardboard for hype, glass for hidden assumptions. Then gravity gets a vote: weak blocks shatter under the weight above them, and the argument stands or falls in front of you.

steel = data stone = sourced wood = assertion cardboard = superlative glass = hidden assumption
