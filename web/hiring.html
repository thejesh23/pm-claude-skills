The Gauntlet trains you to be interviewed. This trains the harder skill: interviewing. A candidate sits down — genuinely strong, polished-but-evasive, or a confident bluffer; you don't know which. Ask your questions, dig where it's soft, then make the call. A hiring coach scores your technique and reveals who you were really talking to.

One or two lines is enough — "Senior PM for our payments platform" works. The candidate's résumé and hidden archetype get generated from it.

🎤 Stage 2 — Your interview

You ask, they answer. Vague answers are an invitation to follow up — the coach is watching whether you accept it.

⚖️ Stage 3 — Your call

Real interviews end with a written recommendation, not a feeling. Make yours before the reveal.
