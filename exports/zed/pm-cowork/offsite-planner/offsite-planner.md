# Offsite Planner Skill

Offsites fail at the extremes: the all-work version (eight hours of conference room that could have been at the office, plus travel) and the all-vibes version (fun had, nothing changed). The honest design starts by *weighting the purposes* — connection, decisions, planning — because they want different formats, and pretending one agenda serves all three equally serves none. Then: sessions designed like workshops (artifacts, not discussions — [workshop-designer](../workshop-designer/SKILL.md) rules), air between them (the corridor conversations are half the value; schedule the corridors), and the follow-through discipline, because an offsite's ROI is measured two weeks later, on Mondays.

## What This Skill Produces

- **The purpose weighting** — connection/decisions/planning split, chosen explicitly with the team's current need
- **The day designs** — session blocks with artifacts, the air between them, the energy arc across days
- **The logistics runbook** — venue, travel, food, the remote-inclusion plan if hybrid, the details that sink offsites when improvised
- **The follow-through kit** — commitments captured with owners/dates, the two-week check, and the "what we decided" note to the wider org

## Required Inputs

Ask for these if not provided:
- **The team's current truth** — new team (connection-heavy)? Post-reorg (alignment)? Strategy fog (decisions)? The weighting follows the actual need, not the standard template
- **The constraints** — budget band, days, travel realities, and who's remote (a hybrid offsite that treats dial-ins as an afterthought damages exactly the connection it exists to build)
- **The decisions in scope** — if decisions are claimed, which ones, with their pre-reads ([decision-meeting-format](../decision-meeting-format/SKILL.md) applies — offsites don't exempt decisions from needing options)
- **Last offsite's autopsy** — what worked, what evaporated; the follow-through design patches the specific evaporation

## Framework: The Design Rules

1. **Weight the purposes out loud:** "60% connection, 30% planning, 10% decisions" is a design; "a bit of everything" is a schedule. The weighting decides the venue (connection wants walks and meals; decisions want a good room), the ratio of session-to-air, and what "success" means — and the team is told the weighting, because expectations misaligned with design produce the "waste of time" verdict regardless of quality.
2. **Sessions produce artifacts; air produces the rest:** every work block ends with a thing (the map, the ranked list, the draft) — and between blocks, real air: long lunches, walks, the unstructured evening. The scheduled-solid offsite exports the office to a nicer room; the corridors are where the trust that justifies travel gets built.
3. **Decisions get offsite-grade prep, not offsite-grade improvisation:** deciding "while we're all here" without pre-reads produces decisions that unravel on re-entry — the in-scope decisions ship their options docs before travel, and the offsite provides the debate and the call.
4. **Logistics are a runbook because failures compound:** the room that fits, food that arrives, travel that works, the hybrid rig tested the day before — each detail is small and their failures multiply against the per-hour cost of the assembled team ([meeting-cost-meter](../meeting-cost-meter/SKILL.md) math, times travel). One owner runs the runbook; the facilitator facilitates.
5. **The offsite ends twice:** once in the room — the last session captures every commitment with owner and date, plus the one-paragraph "what we aligned on" note — and once two weeks later, when the check-in asks what's moved. Offsites without the second ending converge to the same annual ritual: great energy, no deltas, shrinking budgets.

## Output Format

# Offsite: [team] — [days], purpose split: [X/Y/Z%]

## The Weighting (told to the team)
[The split + the why from the team's current truth · what success means under it]

## The Days
| Block | Purpose served | Format → artifact | Air after |
|---|---|---|---|
[Energy arc: heavy work early-day, decisions pre-fatigue, connection where it belongs]

## Logistics Runbook
[Venue/room fit · travel · food · the hybrid rig + test · owner: (name)]

## The Two Endings
[Last session: commitments (owner+date) + the alignment note · T+2 weeks: the check-in on movement, calendared now]

## Quality Checks

- [ ] The purpose weighting is explicit and was communicated
- [ ] Every work block ends in an artifact; air is scheduled, not residual
- [ ] In-scope decisions have pre-reads shipped before travel
- [ ] Logistics have a single named owner and the hybrid setup was tested
- [ ] The T+2 check-in exists on calendars before the offsite starts

## Anti-Patterns

- [ ] Do not schedule solid — the corridors are half the ROI; protect them on purpose
- [ ] Do not improvise decisions because everyone's present — presence isn't preparation
- [ ] Do not treat remote attendees as speakerphones — hybrid inclusion is designed or it's exclusion with a call link
- [ ] Do not let commitments live in the room's memory — owner, date, note, or it was a retreat
- [ ] Do not skip the weighting conversation — mismatched expectations turn good offsites into "wastes of time" in the retelling
